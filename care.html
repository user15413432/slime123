SLIME CARE
GUIDE

Learn how to keep your slime fresh, stretchy, and satisfying for months to come. Proper care extends the life of your sensory experience.

Room Temperature

01

PROPER STORAGE

Always keep your slime in an airtight container when not in use. Exposure to air will cause it to dry out and lose its stretchy texture. Store at room temperature away from direct sunlight.

Pro Tip: Use the original container or a similar airtight jar. Avoid plastic bags as they can leak air over time.

02

REHYDRATION

If your slime becomes stiff or sticky, it's time to rehydrate. Add a few drops of warm water or slime activator and knead thoroughly until the desired consistency returns.

Pro Tip: Start with 2-3 drops and add more as needed. Too much water can make slime runny.

03

CLEAN HANDS, CLEAN SLIME

Always wash and dry your hands before playing with slime. Dirt, lint, and oils from your skin can contaminate the slime and affect its texture. This also prevents bacteria growth.

Warning: Never play with slime if your hands are dirty or greasy. It can ruin the slime permanently.

04

TEMPERATURE MATTERS

Keep slime at room temperature (18-22°C). Extreme heat can melt it, while cold temperatures can make it hard and brittle. Never leave slime in a car or near heating sources.

Pro Tip: If slime gets too cold, let it warm up to room temperature before playing. Don't microwave!

CARE BY TEXTURE

Glossy Slime

Add a few drops of glycerin to restore shine. Avoid over-activating as it reduces glossiness.

Butter Slime

Knead regularly to maintain soft, spreadable texture. Add lotion if it becomes too stiff.

Fluffy Slime

Don't over-knead or it will deflate. Store with extra space in container to preserve volume.
